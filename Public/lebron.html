About Lebron

LeBron James, born December 30, 1984, Akron, Ohio, U.S. is an American professional basketball player who is widely considered one of the greatest all-around players of all time. Lebron won National Basketball Association (NBA) championships with the Miami Heat (2012 and 2013), the Cleveland Cavaliers (2016), and the Los Angeles Lakers (2020).

Lebron's Career

Nicknamed "King James", he is widely recognized as one of the best players in the sport's history and is often compared with Michael Jordan in debates on the greatest basketball player of all time. In addition to being the NBA's all-time leading scorer and ranking fourth in career assists, James holds several individual honors: four Most Valuable Player (MVP) Awards, four Finals MVP Awards, three All-Star Game MVP Awards, and the inaugural NBA Cup MVP award.

Lebron's Legacy

Lebron's legacy carries on through what he's remembered by, his children, and being one of the best basketball players to ever live. Lebron has a wife and 3 children, his wife's name is Savannah James, and his children's names are Bronny, Bryce, and Zhuri. Bronny is 20 and Bryce is 17. Bronny is in the NBA as well, playing on the same team as his dad, while Bryce is playing on his high school team, preparing to play pro for college or go to the NBA.
